ACCESSIBILITY NOTES — Dr. Marina Capella Homepage v4.3
Peaceful Media · Brand-to-Web Strategy Workshop · June 2026

WHAT WE DID
Every color combination on the page was checked against WCAG 2.1 AA,
the standard accessibility benchmark for the web:
  · 4.5:1 minimum contrast for normal text
  · 3.0:1 for large text (24px+ / 19px bold)
  · 3.0:1 for UI parts like input borders and focus rings

THE HEADLINE
Marina's approved 5-color palette stays exactly as approved. Every hue
is unchanged — we only deepened the SHADE in the handful of places
where small text sits directly on a color, the same way a print shop
runs the same ink heavier on a textured stock. Big display moments
(the sage italic phrases in headlines, the stat numerals) keep the
original swatch values because large text passes at the lighter shade.

WHAT CHANGED AND WHY (all ratios verified programmatically)

1. Terracotta CTAs & links — #A36B51 → #8A5238 (hover #74452F)
   Button labels were 3.62:1 and text links 3.26:1 (both fail).
   Now 5.17:1 and 4.66:1 (pass). Same clay warmth, one step deeper.
   The original #A36B51 remains in the palette for large display use.

2. Small green text on cream — sage #667B57 → deep sage #526547
   Eyebrow labels, card audience tags, the pull-quote, and the logo's
   italic "Capella" were 3.43:1 (fail at their sizes). Now 4.70:1.
   Large sage display text (headline italics, stat numerals) is
   unchanged — at those sizes 3.43:1 passes AA.

3. Green panels (video / speaking / contact) — #667B57 → #526547
   Cream body text on the sage panels was 3.12–3.81:1 (fail).
   On the deepened panel it reads at 4.70–5.22:1 (pass). The panels
   read as the same brand green, simply richer.

4. Contact form fields — translucent → solid cream fields
   The see-through fields failed three ways: typed text 3.14:1,
   placeholder 1.98:1, border 1.83:1. Solid cream-card fields with
   forest text now measure 11.69:1 / 4.64:1 / 5.22:1.

5. Focus indicators — ochre ring → context-aware rings
   The ochre keyboard-focus ring was nearly invisible on cream
   (1.76:1). Focus is now deep forest on light sections (10.53:1)
   and cream on dark sections (4.70:1+), so keyboard users can
   always see where they are.

6. Hero text protection — engineered scrim
   The photo overlay now guarantees at least a 74%-opacity forest
   scrim behind the headline zone. Even if the photo were pure white
   in that area, cream text would measure 5.83:1. On phones the
   scrim runs full-width at a 72% floor (5.08:1 worst case).

7. Small fixes
   · Book signup field border: 1.28:1 → 3.43:1
   · Footer fine print: 4.00:1 → 6.14:1
   · Form routing hints: now solid cream chips, links at 5.17:1
   · "Get in touch" hover (v4.3): a nav rule was recoloring the
     button label dark-on-dark on hover; the label now stays cream
     at 6.56:1
   · Speaking topic numerals (v4.3): cream on the deep-sage panel,
     4.70:1

STRUCTURE & SCREEN READERS (also in v4.2)
   · "Skip to main content" link for keyboard users
   · Proper <main> landmark; single h1; logical heading order
   · Mobile menu button labeled and wired with aria-controls
   · Form routing hints announce politely to screen readers
   · Decorative icons hidden from assistive tech
   · All animation already respects "reduce motion" settings

WHAT STILL PASSES UNCHANGED
Forest text on cream (10.53:1 — AAA), footer text (8.36:1), all
headings, ochre hairline dividers (decorative, exempt), large sage
display text, and the white-on-terracotta… everything not listed
above was checked and already compliant.

— Verified with WCAG 2.1 relative-luminance math, including
  alpha-composited colors (e.g. text at reduced opacity).
