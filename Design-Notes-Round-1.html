Your homepage.
The thinking behind it.

A walk through how we translated everything you shared with us into a page that feels like you — so that when you sit with it, you'll know not just what we built, but why each choice is here.

See it live: mc.peacefuldesign.com This is: Your homepage, first concept (Round 1) Where we are: Roadmap call #2

Marina —

When we first sat down, you told us the hardest thing wasn't making a website look nice. It was holding all of you on one page — the integrative physician, the mentor, the community builder — three identities you were clear are integrated, not separate. You'd also been burned once before, by a team that never quite captured your vision.

So before we talk about color or type, we want one thing to be true: this should feel like you saw your own words reflected back. Almost every decision below traces directly to something you said in our calls — and we've put your own lines beside them so you can see the thread.

— Nicki & Sable

01

One you — many avenues

You don't fit in one box, and we didn't try to squeeze you into one. The page opens with who you are, then fans out into clearly-labeled paths — so each visitor sorts themselves in seconds and heads to the right place.

In discovery, you said
"Depending on what people are wanting, these are the avenues."
  • Three avenues. For physicians routes to DPC Pediatrician, for future doctors to Future Minority Doctor, for families to Healing Arts Pediatrics — each its own front door, all under one roof.
  • Physicians seeking coaching are the priority guest. You named them as audience number one, so the page is weighted toward them — but you're introduced as a whole person first, never "coach here, sign up."
  • The ask deepens as you earn the scroll. A soft invitation (the book list) sits up top; bigger commitments — coaching, speaking, becoming a patient — come further down, once trust is built.
02

Healing the healer — soul, with the mastery intact

The line you loved leads the whole page: "Healing the healer. Reimagining medicine." You asked for more soul than clinical — personal and story-first — but you were just as clear that your mastery has to land. As a woman of color in medicine, you've earned every letter after your name, and you shouldn't be shy about them.

In discovery, you said
"It's not just my credentials… I want the soul injected into that too."
  • Two-part headlines. A grounded statement, then an italic "soul" turn — "A book for every healer who forgot they were one." It's the signature device that carries your voice across the page.
  • Credentials stated plainly, then humanized. The facts are right there — double board-certified; MD, MEd, MBA, FAAP, FABOIM; Stanford and Harvard — but the story does the emotional work.
  • Integration is the through-line. The recurring idea is the one you named as your superpower: uniting the left and right brain — the microscope and the forest, science and soul.
03

Warm & earthy — sophisticated, never sterile

You described your tones without hesitation, and pointed at the greens you were wearing. So the palette is a disciplined, grounded five — built from your approved swatch. The governing rule: cream used as the primary base, never stark white, so the page reads like paper and skin, not a clinic waiting room.

In discovery, you said
"Warm and earthy feels right for me… I feel a really strong connection to nature."

04

The science and the soul — in the type itself

You told us you loved the blend of a literary serif with a clean, easy-to-read sans — letters with a little intuition in them, paired with letters that simply get out of the way. That duet became your thesis expressed in type.

Fraunces · the soul
Healing the healer.
Reimagining medicine.
A warm serif with true italics — it carries every headline and pull-quote, and the italic always signals the emotional turn.
Figtree · the clarity
Calm, friendly body copy that never competes with the headline — generous spacing, a comfortable line length, easy on every screen.
Clear and trustworthy — it does the plain-spoken work so the serif can do the feeling.

One family for feeling, one for clarity. That's the left brain and the right, sitting on the page together — exactly the integration you keep coming back to.

05

Room to breathe — clean, never busy

You were specific: to the point, not too much visual distraction, balanced white space. So the page paces itself — airy cream sections alternate with a few deep-sage panels that give your most emotional moments a quieter, darker room to land in.

In discovery, you said
"I like it more to the point, clear. Not too much visual distraction."
  • A full-bleed hero photograph. Your real brand portrait fills the top of the page, with you facing into the headline; a precision-tuned scrim keeps the words perfectly readable over the image at any screen size.
  • A soft top-light wash warms the top of each cream section and settles back down — a subtle breath of light that only ever goes lighter, so it never makes text harder to read.
  • One quiet thread of ornament. A small ochre diamond and hairline rule recur down the page, stitching every section into a single identity rather than a stack of unrelated blocks.
06

In your own words — video at the center

You said something that shaped this whole section: that video carries a frequency a still photo can't. And you're comfortable on camera — 200-plus podcast episodes is no small thing. So we built a calm, deliberate home for film right in the middle of the page.

In discovery, you said
"Video transmits that frequency in a way that just static images don't."
  • A grounding sage band with a single click-to-play frame — quiet, unhurried, so your voice is the thing that fills the room.
  • A lighter second clip. Your Defying Gravity DPC parody sits just below as "a lighter note" — because reimagining medicine can start with a little joy, and it shows a side of you people fall for.
  • The play buttons were softened on purpose — smaller and semi-transparent, so they invite a click without ever covering your face.
07

Your gatekeeper — contact, on your terms

You were honest that you're already drowning in email and wanted something that sorts inquiries before they reach you. So the contact form opens with one question — "I'm reaching out about…" — and adapts to the answer.

In discovery, you said
"I want some support email that can be my gatekeeper. I'm already overwhelmed with email as it is."
  • It routes people for you. Choose "becoming a patient" or "DPC consulting" and a gentle note points them straight to the right property — often answering them before they ever hit send.
  • Speaking inquiries come here too. Just as you wanted: one central place to field speaking requests, so you never have to bolt a speaking page onto every other site.
  • Your team gets pre-sorted notes; visitors get an instant sense of direction. Less noise for you, a clearer path for them.
08

The book, and the list — start building now

Your second priority was clear: start growing the mailing list ahead of the book, so there's an audience waiting when it arrives. So a calm signup anchors the lower page, paired with a typographic cover concept for Awakening the Healer's Soul.

  • A soft, low-pressure invitation — "join the list to follow the journey" — with microcopy that promises no noise, just meaningful updates. It matches your voice, not a hard sell.
  • The cover is a concept, not final art — a placeholder that holds the shape and feeling until the real cover exists, so you can share the link with family, friends, and colleagues today.
09

Quietly excellent — accessible, and built to last

Two things you'll never see directly, but will feel. First, the page is genuinely accessible — for a healer's brand, that's a values statement, not a checkbox. Second, it's built simply and durably, so you're never locked into anyone.

  • Readable for everyone. Every text-and-color pairing was measured against the WCAG AA standard rather than eyeballed — so the page treats every reader with the same care you give a patient.
  • Calm by default. Gentle motion guides the eye as you scroll, and anyone who prefers less movement gets a complete, still page — never a blank screen waiting on an animation.
  • Plain, portable, yours. It's built as straightforward web files — fast to load, cheap to host, and easy to hand to your team. Given your last experience, that independence matters: nothing here traps you.
10

What's here now — and what's still to come

So you review with the right lens, here's exactly what's finished and what's still a placeholder dropping into a ready-made slot:

  • Photography — your brand shoot is in. The hero, your story portrait, and the speaking portrait are your real images, placed and color-tuned to the palette.
  • Video — still interim. The current clip is a stand-in; your final brand film, produced with a video team, drops into the same frame when it's ready.
  • Testimonials — real, and growing. The patient quotes are genuine. Physician-coaching and mentee testimonials are still to be gathered.
  • Book cover — a concept. A typographic stand-in, not the final cover art.
Everything that makes this page yours — the structure, the voice, the palette, the feeling — is ready for your eyes. Review the feeling; the final assets settle into finished slots.
