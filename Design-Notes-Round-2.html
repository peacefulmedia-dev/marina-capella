The new model of medicine.
Round two of your homepage.

After our last call — and the conversation with Sonia that gave you permission to think bigger — you asked us to be bolder. This round threads one idea through the whole page: that you aren't only healing physicians, you're building a new model of medicine. Here's what changed, and why.

Marina —

Last time, you told us something had shifted. "Healing the healer" still resonated, but you'd realized it pointed at only one part of the work — your work with physicians. What you're actually reaching for is larger: to reinvent medicine itself. A model that brings the soul back to the science, for healers and the people they care for alike.

So this round isn't a redesign — it's a refocus. The voice, palette, and type you loved are all intact. What's new is a clear north star, named plainly, and woven through every section so the page finally says the bold thing out loud. Below, each change sits beside the words of yours that prompted it.

— Nicki & Sable

01

A bolder north star — the new model of medicine

The lead message changed. "Healing the healer" now lives further down as a thread, not the banner — because, as you put it, it spoke only to physicians. The page now opens on the bigger vision, the one that includes everyone you want to reach.

On our last call, you said
"The problem with that is it focuses only on my work with physicians — whereas what I'm really trying to do is reinvent medicine. Create a new model for everyone."
Round 1 hero

Healing the healer. Reimagining medicine.

Round 2 hero

Welcome to the new model of medicine. With a primary button that says Explore the new model.

  • The opening paragraph now speaks to physicians first — coaching and mentorship to heal from burnout and relearn the intuitive, soul-centered side of medicine training left out — then widens to the whole vision.
  • Three headline options are built into the page so you can feel which level of boldness fits before we lock it: "Welcome to the new model of medicine," "Reimagining medicine, starting with the healer," and "Integrative Medicine 2.0."
02

A new section at the heart — where the idea lives

The page now has a section that does nothing but name the thing you're building. It's a quiet, deep-sage band — your most important idea given its own room — headed "You mastered the science. Now reclaim the soul of medicine."

On our last call, you said
"This is the new model of medicine. This is medicine 2.0 — reimagining medicine."
  • It names the gap you keep returning to: training taught you the science and told you to silence your intuition. This is the half of a physician's education nobody gave them — and the page says so directly.
  • Three pillars carry the promise: Relearn your intuition · Science and soul, integrated · Heal the healer first — styled to match the "For physicians" columns, so the page feels like one system.
  • It coins the term. The section closes on "Call it Integrative Medicine 2.0," set in your signature italic so the phrase lands as a banner you can own.
03

Saying it out loud — you're leading this

You said the bold framing still feels uncomfortable — and also that you don't see anyone else doing what you're envisioning. That space is yours to claim. The copy steps into it plainly, without slipping into hype.

On our last call, you said
"I don't really see anyone out there doing what I am envisioning. So there is space to be filled — to own that space."
  • The page positions you as the one building the model — "I'm building a new model of medicine that gives physicians back the other half of their education" — first person, grounded, never a sales pitch.
  • It stays true to your voice. Bold in claim, calm in tone. We lead with the vision, not with "leader of" language you're not ready to wear yet — the boldness is in what you're doing, stated simply.
04

Keeping people home — the avenues now point inward

In Round 1, the three avenue cards sent visitors straight back out to your other sites. You wanted the opposite: a hub that holds people here. So the cards now lead down the page — to the vision, to coaching, to the book — your three most important topics.

On our last call, you said
"If our intent is to keep people on the site… instead of leading externally, we could lead down to the section on the page."

Round 2 avenues

  • The other businesses are still reachable — through the contact gatekeeper and the footer — but the umbrella site is now the destination, not a waystation.
05

The book, brought forward — the bridge to the message

You were clear that the book is what carries this whole idea to the world. So it's been given a real moment of its own, lower on the page where it bridges your story to the bigger vision.

On our last call, you said
"The book is really going to be the bridge to that message… maybe making the book more prominent."
  • A standing book cover — the "Dawn" concept — a rising sun on warm cream, in terracotta and gold. It reads as hopeful and literary, an uplifting memoir, not a textbook.
  • It sits on a quieter, deeper-cream band so it stands apart from your story above it and feels like its own held moment.
  • The mailing-list signup lives right here — your stated second priority, building the audience now so there's a room full of people waiting when the book lands. The microcopy keeps your promise: no noise, just meaningful updates.
  • The cover is a concept, not final art — a placeholder holding the shape and feeling until the real cover exists.
