What Marina is expecting

  • A live, clickable homepage — not WordPress yet, an interactive concept she can scroll and click.
  • To go a level deeper into granularity than the discovery call.
  • That she doesn't have to give all her feedback at once — we told her she can sit with it after.
  • A calm, soul-forward presence that feels like her — she's wary after being burned by a past agency.
  • To leave inspired and empowered — the feeling she said she wants visitors to have.

Before we dial in

  • Staging loaded & warm: mc.peacefuldesign.com — and the share screen ready on the hero.
  • Have the client Design Notes open in case she wants the "why" behind a choice.
  • Know the asset status cold: photos in, video interim, testimonials partial, book cover a concept.
  • Keep her own words handy ("avenues", "healing the healer", "more soul") — mirror her language.
  • Confirm we won't run WordPress/CRM through her VA — her team keeps that; we handle this umbrella site's list.

Run of show

~75 min of 90 · leave air to breathe
0:005 min

Reconnect, warmly

She's coming meeting-to-meeting on a non-clinical day. Keep it human for a beat before screens go up — this is a relationship, not a demo.

Say"Before we dive in — we built this entirely from what you told us. You'll recognize a lot of your own words." Sets the frame: she was heard.
0:055 min

Frame what she's looking at

Set expectations so feedback lands in the right place: this is an interactive concept, not the final hosted site; some assets are placeholders; this is iterative — another pass comes after.

Say"React to the feeling and the structure first. Don't worry about a typo or a stand-in photo — note it and move on."
0:1035 min

Walk the page, top to bottom

Scroll slowly. For each section, point out what it's doing and why (tie back to her words), then pause and listen. Don't rush to the next one.

  • Hero — "Healing the healer." Her tagline, her real brand photo, scrim for legibility. Listen: does the line still land? Does she feel "her" in the photo?
  • Avenues. Three paths sorting visitors — her word. Listen: are the labels and order right? Physicians first?
  • My story. Soul-forward, first-person, credentials humanized. Listen: soul ↔ mastery balance — too much, too little?
  • Video. Calm sage band + the lighter Defying Gravity clip. Listen: reaction to the parody; appetite for the brand film.
  • For physicians / coaching. Her priority audience. Listen: does it speak to a burned-out doctor?
  • Speaking. Routed centrally, as she asked. Listen: topics accurate? anything to add?
  • The book + mailing list. Her #2 priority. Listen: confirm title & subtitle; signup copy feel right?
  • Testimonials. Real patient quotes. Listen: which physician/mentee quotes can she get us?
  • Contact gatekeeper. One question, self-routing. Listen: are the intent options complete?
0:4510 min

"Does it feel like you?"

Step back from the pixels. This is the question that matters most for her — and the one her last agency missed.

Questions that feed the Session 3 redesign

Adapted from our ICI strategy framework, but reframed for a concept review: we're not discovering from scratch, we're confirming and refining what's on the page. Each theme has something to ask and something to listen for — her answers here become next week's design brief.

1 · Homepage jobs

AskOf the four jobs — present you holistically, grow the book list, gatekeep contact, field speaking — which must the homepage do best this season?

ListenWhich job she keeps circling back to; anything she expected to see and didn't.

2 · Audience pathways

AskFor each avenue, is the next step obvious and correct? Should physicians-seeking-coaching sit even more front-and-center?

ListenHesitation on any routing; whether the families-vs-physicians priority still holds; the book / non-physician visitor.

3 · Core message & how direct

AskHow directly should we name burnout, trauma in training, "the system breaks its healers"? Any of her personal story (immigrant roots, her own depression) to surface — or keep private?

ListenWords to embrace or avoid; where to set the soul ↔ credentials dial.

4 · Ecosystem visibility

AskWhat gets foregrounded vs. sits deeper — the DPC podcast (200+ eps), the three brands, the book, speaking?

ListenAnything too prominent or buried. (She's already said the FMD podcast isn't homepage-worthy — confirm that still holds.)

5 · Trust & credibility

AskWhich proof points feel accurate and aligned — credentials, affiliations, testimonials, episode count, faculty roles? Quantitative or qualitative?

ListenHer discomfort with "bragging" against her own point that, as a woman of color, she needs the credibility to be taken seriously.

6 · Collect from her

GetPhysician / mentee testimonials · final book subtitle · brand-video story angle · any added credibility logos or affiliations.

ConfirmSign-off on the brand-shoot photos and the "Latino Physicians of Utah" wording.

Handle with care

  • The past burn. She lost $15k to an agency that didn't capture her vision. Our throughline all call: we listened, and here's the proof. Lead with her own words.
  • Cost-conscious. She spends hard-earned money carefully. Frame extras as options, never assumed scope — don't over-promise.
  • Soul ↔ credentials. The trickiest balance she named. If she wants a nudge either way, we can tune it — but the page already leads with soul and keeps the pedigree.
  • Her voice is the copy. Invite refinements, not a blank-slate rewrite. The two-part "soul turn" headlines are the device — protect them.
  • Small correction to verify: the affiliations strip now reads "Latino Physicians of Utah" — confirm that's the name she wants.
  • Keep it unhurried. She's a slow, intuitive processor and so is their strategist — silence is fine; let her sit with things.

Decisions to land by end of call

  1. Tagline — confirm "Healing the healer. Reimagining medicine." stays the hero line.
  2. Book — confirm title Awakening the Healer's Soul and lock a subtitle.
  3. Mailing list — direction on platform (we run it for this umbrella site, not her VA).
  4. Video — green-light the brand film with our video team; confirm story angle.
  5. Photography — sign-off on the brand-shoot images; does she like the photos we chose, and is each one placed right (hero, story, speaking)?
  6. Color palette — confirm the warm-and-earthy five (forest, sage, ochre, cream, terracotta) feels like "her color."
  7. Copy density — too much copy anywhere? Not enough? Which sections to trim or expand.
  8. Her voice — the big one: did we land it? Does the page sound like her, soul-forward with the mastery intact?
  9. Speaking — confirm the central-form approach and topic list.
